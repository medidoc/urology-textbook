Thoracoabdominal Incision for Laparotomy: Surgical Steps


Thoracoabdominal incision: from the 7th intercostal space to the costal arch, then followed by a paramedian or median laparotomy down to the umbilicus.

Urologic Indications for a Thoracoabdominal Laparotomy

Indications for a thoracoabdominal incision are radical nephrectomy or adrenalectomy for huge tumors, renal carcinoma with caval thrombus, and retroperitoneal lymphadenectomy for bulky disease with retrocrural lymph node metastases.

Patient positioning:

Supine position with hyperextension of the lumbar spine, lifting the ipsilateral shoulder to 30 degrees (oblique positioning pillows) and hanging the ipsilateral arm to a bracket with 90 degrees anteversion in the shoulder joint and 90 degrees of flexion in the elbow joint.

Surgical Technique of a Thoracoabdominal Incision

  • Skin incision see fig. thoracoabdominal incision: from the 7th intercostal space to the costal arch, then following a paramedian or median line down to the umbilicus.
  • Incise the trunk muscles to the 8th rib (latissimus dorsi, serratus anterior, external oblique muscle)
  • Paramedian laparotomy: incise the anterior lamina of the rectus sheath. The rectus abdominis is dissected below the costal arch and the muscle is pushed laterally. Open the posterior lamina of the rectus sheath and the peritoneum.
  • A median incision of the abdomen is also possible.
  • Cut the intercostal muscles in the 7th intercostal space at the cranial margin of the 8th rib. Open the pleural cavity at the costal arch. The costal arch is transsected with a rib shear.
  • A thoracic retractor is used to spread the intercostal space, an additional retractor is used for the laparotomy. The diaphragm is divided 2 cm from the costal origin (to avoid the larger branches of the phrenic nerve), until sufficient exposure of the retroperitoneum is achieved (Lumsden et al., 1993).

Wound Closure after Thoracoabdominal Incision

Begin with interrupted sutures of the diaphragm, including all layers (pleura, muscle, and peritoneum). Insert a chest tube through the 8th intercostal space and a separate stab incision. Pericostal stitches with vicryl 1 are placed around the ribs (7 and 8) and loosely tied to close the chest. The costal arch is sutured with vicryl 1 or nonabsorbable material. The laparotomy is closed as described in the section midline laparotomy. The serratus anterior and latissimus dorsi muscles are sutured with interrupted stitches.
