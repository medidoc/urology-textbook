Diseases of the Ureter

Anatomy of the Ureters

The ureter is a 22–30 cm long muscular tube, which connects renal pelvis and bladder.
  • Anatomy of the ureter and renal pelvis

Congenital Diseases of the Ureter

  • Ureteropelvic junction obstruction
  • Duplex kidney or duplicated ureter
  • Ectopic ureter
  • Ureterocele
  • Vesicoureteral reflux
  • Megaureter
  • Retrocaval ureter
  • Retroiliac ureter
  • Ureteral diverticula, ureteral valves, ureteral stenosis, ureteral torsion
  • Extrarenal pyelocalyceal system
Retrograde pyelography of a UPJ obstruction.

Ureteral Trauma

  • Injury of the ureter
  • Iatrogenic injury of the ureter

Benign Diseases of the Ureter

  • Ureteral stones
  • Ureteral stricture
  • Retroperitoneal fibrosis (Morbus Ormond)
  • Ureteritis cystica

Malignant Tumors of the Ureter

  • Upper Tract Urothelial Cancer

Surgical Procedures for Ureteral Diseases

  • Nephroureterectomy
  • Open pyeloplasty
  • Laparoscopic pyeloplasty
  • Ureteroureterostomy
  • Ureterocystoneostomy
  • Megaureter operations
  • Ureteroscopy
